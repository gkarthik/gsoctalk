the proposal

  1. Making the proposal.
  2. Get possible mentors to review it(*).
  3. Incorporate and go to to step 2. 

*Assuming that you've talked to mentors before the application period begins.

making the proposal

Sections
  • Title, Name & Contact
  • Synopsis
  • Features to be expected at the end of summer
  • Timeline -> Preferably at a week's interval
  • Benefits to community
  • What you hope to learn
  • Other commitments during the summer
  • Links to demos and mock ups**
                                                                 ** Important
I had listed out links to my demo almost five times throughout my proposal.

Ask your possible mentor to review it beforehand

got through?

Congratulations! You just earned 500$.

  • Set up scheduled calls with your mentor.
  • Push code regularly and never lose touch with your mentor.
  • Update documentation as and when you push code. Make sure your commit messages are understandable.
  • Maintain a log of your progress, to be shared with your mentor or the world!
  • Remember those annoying #comments? You need these. Your code is going to be read and reviewed by a community of hackers.

mid term evaluations

If you are in regular touch with your mentor, these should be a breeze.

  • Update your code and documentation before your evaluations.
  • Remember to fill in the forms that Google asks you to, ON TIME.
  • Talk to your mentor regarding your evaluations, if need be.

Finances
Mid term evaluations - $2250 + $5

the final commit

Before your final evaluations, if you have stuck to your timeline, you should not have any issues.

If you are lagging,  CODE BETTER AND FASTER.
Make sure you implement all the proposed features.

Contact your mentor in relation to what code samples you wanna send Google.
